KIM JUNG GI
Kim Jung Gi is a Korean artist born in 1975. He was 19 when he entered the Fine Arts School, where he majored in “Art & Design”. His first publication was published in the magazine Young Jump which is the most well-known Japanese magazine. He went on to illustrate 2 novels written by the French author Bernard WERBER: “Paradise” (in 2010) and “Third humanity” (in 2013). Whatever the angle, his illustrations are always perfect; his knowledge of human and animal anatomy is that incredible. He is in the Guinness World Records, in the Illustration category under “Longest drawing by an individual”, a record he broke doing Fisheye art.
